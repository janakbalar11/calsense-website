Clinical & Diabetic Care

Catching diabetic foot ulcers before they start

Diabetic foot ulcers (DFU) are a preventable crisis with an outsized human and economic cost. CalSense extends continuous risk monitoring beyond the clinic, into daily life.

Prevention

The goal: diagnose before Stage 2

Early-stage tissue damage often goes completely undetected by the patient. Without intervention, the ulcer becomes deeper and increasingly likely to become infected — the point at which outcomes worsen sharply.

"DFU can be avoided if detected at an early stage." — the core premise behind continuous monitoring.

The Access Gap

A shortage of hands, not a shortage of solutions

Provider Shortage

Risk-factor assessment is typically carried out by trained health workers who are in limited supply.

Disruptive Visits

Patients are forced to schedule disruptive or expensive doctor visits just to get a routine risk check.

Limited Self-Exam

Many patients have limited physical ability to examine their own feet independently, especially with reduced mobility.

Self-Monitoring, Enabled

Continuous, wearable monitoring closes the gap between clinic visits — surfacing risk in real time, at home.

The Solution

Continuous monitoring that fits into daily life

The CalSense insole slides into a patient's existing shoes and continuously tracks plantar pressure distribution — transmitting data wirelessly to a mobile app that surfaces feedback to relieve pressure before an ulcer can form.
